Construction loans advance in draws against verified construction milestones — not as a lump sum. A Quantity Surveyor (QS) or Cost Consultant inspects work-in-place and certifies each draw. The lender advances the lesser of: (a) the QS-certified draw amount, or (b) the LTC-governed maximum at that stage. Interest is charged only on drawn funds.

Construction hold codes are funding blockers — conditions that must be satisfied before the lender will advance funds. They fall into two categories: pre-funding holds (must clear before initial advance) and draw-specific holds (must clear before each subsequent draw). Hard holds are non-negotiable. Soft holds can be resolved with a credit memo and committee approval.
🔴 Pre-Funding Hard Holds — Must Clear Before Initial Advance
Financial & Structure
HCF-01 Cost Proforma Verified: Total Project Cost reconciled by lender's Quantity Surveyor (QS) or Cost Consultant. QS report must confirm hard costs within ±10% of borrower's budget. Lender will not advance if QS identifies material cost understatement.
HCF-02 Equity Injection Confirmed: All equity (cash, land equity, or equity takeout) must be documented and in trust or verified by solicitor before first advance. Equity must be in prior to or simultaneous with first draw. No equity = no advance.
HCF-03 Interest Reserve Funded: Full interest reserve must be in place at closing — either funded upfront in trust or committed as a holdback from available loan proceeds. A shortfall in the interest reserve is a hard stop.
HCF-04 Source of Funds — Equity: Bank statements, wire confirmations, or title documentation confirming the origin of equity funds. Anti-money-laundering (FINTRAC) compliance — unexplained deposits must be cleared.
HCF-05 Pre-Sale / Pre-Lease Threshold: If lender requires pre-sales (banks typically require 65–70% for condo), signed presale contracts with deposits must be verified. Condo sales must be reviewed by lender's counsel for validity and enforceability.
Legal & Security
HCL-01 Title Clear: Land title search confirming no registered encumbrances, liens, or caveats that rank in priority to the construction mortgage. Any existing mortgage must be discharged at closing or subordinated. Title insurance may be required.
HCL-02 Mortgage Registered: Construction mortgage (or collateral charge) registered in first position on title in BC Land Title and Survey Authority. PPSA filing on personal property (equipment, materials) if applicable.
HCL-03 Personal Guarantee(s) Executed: Signed and witnessed personal guarantees from all principals (with >25% ownership). For multi-entity structures: guarantee from parent company, operating company, and individuals. Corporate resolution authorizing borrowing required.
HCL-04 Assignment of Contracts: Assignment of the GC contract, architect agreement, and all key subcontracts to the lender as additional security. Allows lender to step in and complete the project if the borrower defaults.
HCL-05 Assignment of Pre-Sales: If applicable — assignment of all presale contracts and deposits to lender as additional security. Deposits must be held in trust by lender's solicitor or in a designated trust account.
HCL-06 Solicitor's Reporting Letter: Lender's lawyer must confirm: title clear, security registered, all conditions satisfied, no known claims, documents in order. This letter is the final gate before initial advance.
Collateral & Property
HCC-01 AACI Appraisal — As-Is + As-Complete: Full appraisal by AACI-designated appraiser confirming (a) current as-is land value and (b) as-complete market value supporting the GDV assumption. LTC and LTV calculations are both based on this appraisal — not borrower's estimates.
HCC-02 Environmental — Phase I: Phase I Environmental Site Assessment required on all construction sites. If Phase I identifies areas of concern, Phase II is required and must come back clean before advance. Environmental holdback may be required if remediation risk exists.
HCC-03 Builder's Risk Insurance: Course of Construction (Builder's Risk) policy with lender named as loss payee and additional insured. General liability minimum $5M. Policy must be in force before work commences. Proof of premium payment required.
HCC-04 GC Insurance & Bonding: GC must provide Certificate of Insurance (COI) with lender as additional insured. Performance bond and labour/material payment bond may be required (typically on deals >$5M or for less experienced GCs).
HCC-05 Building Permit: City-issued building permit must be in hand at time of initial advance (or as a condition of advance if lender approved at DP stage). Lender will not advance without confirmed permit — no permit = no project.
Development & Compliance
HCD-01 GC Contract — Fixed Price: Signed, fixed-price (or guaranteed maximum price — GMP) GC contract. Lenders will not advance against a cost-plus contract without a GMP cap. Related-party GC requires additional scrutiny — arm's-length review of pricing.
HCD-02 Draw Schedule Approved: Project draw schedule signed off by lender, QS, and borrower before initial advance. Schedule must align with construction milestones and QS reporting cadence. Changes to the draw schedule require lender consent.
HCD-03 KYC / AML — All Parties: FINTRAC-compliant KYC on all borrowers, guarantors, and beneficial owners. Corporate structure chart required. Any PEP (Politically Exposed Person) flags require escalated due diligence. Anti-money-laundering compliance is non-negotiable.
HCD-04 No Material Adverse Change: Signed NAC declaration from borrower at closing confirming: no litigation commenced, no material change in financial position, no change in project scope, no new encumbrances since commitment letter.
🟡 Draw-Level Holds — Must Clear Before Each Draw Advance
HDR-01 QS Draw Certification: Quantity Surveyor must certify (a) work in place at the claimed percentage, (b) cost to complete within the remaining budget, (c) no cost overruns that would blow the TPC budget. QS certificate is required before each draw after initial advance.
HDR-02 No Builders Liens Registered: Title search at time of each draw confirming no new builders liens registered. Any lien must be vacated (by payment or bond) before draw is released. Unpaid subcontractors are a draw blocker.
HDR-03 Insurance Current: Builder's Risk policy must remain active and in good standing at each draw. Proof of renewal if policy expires mid-construction. Any claim against the policy must be disclosed to lender.
HDR-04 Project Not in Default: Borrower must not be in default under the construction mortgage, GC contract, or any other project agreement. Material deviation from approved plans requires lender consent before next draw is released.
HDR-05 Cost-to-Complete Within Budget: QS must confirm that the estimated cost to complete, added to costs incurred to date, does not exceed the total approved TPC budget. If there is a cost overrun, borrower must fund the shortfall from equity before the next draw.
HDS-01 Soft Hold — Schedule Delay: Construction is behind schedule by >30 days. Not an automatic stop, but triggers a lender-approved construction completion schedule with revised milestones. Requires signed acknowledgment from GC and borrower. Interest reserve may need to be topped up.
HDS-02 Soft Hold — Cost Overrun <10%: Cost overrun is within the contingency budget. Lender will advance with written confirmation from borrower that equity is available to cover the overage. Does not require formal committee approval if within approved contingency threshold.
Op codes define how the construction loan behaves operationally throughout its life — from initial advance through final holdback release and exit. Construction loans are fundamentally different from term loans: they are non-revolving, milestone-funded, and have a defined term ending at stabilization or sale. They do not replace permanent financing — they bridge to it.
Facility Classification

Facility Type

OP-CLConstruction Loan — Non-revolving draw facility. Advances against milestones. Not re-drawable once repaid.
OP-CL-IOInterest-Only During Construction — No principal during draw period. Full balance due at maturity (at completion or exit).
OP-CL-DEVDevelopment Loan — Includes land, pre-construction soft costs, and hard construction advances under one facility.
OP-CL-LANDLand + Construction — Two-phase: land acquisition advance first, then construction draws begin after permit. Common in BC development financing.
OP-BRIDGEBridge-to-Permanent — Construction loan structured to exit into CMHC insured term loan or conventional commercial mortgage upon stabilization.

Repayment Logic

OP-R-IOInterest-Only — Monthly interest charged on outstanding balance only. No principal payments during construction term.
OP-R-SALERepayment by Sale — Loan is repaid from condo/strata sale proceeds at completion. Net proceeds flow through lender's lawyer.
OP-R-REFIRepayment by Refinance — Construction loan repaid by take-out financing (CMHC insured term, credit union permanent, etc.) within loan term.
OP-R-BALLOONBalloon at Maturity — Full principal + accrued interest due at end of construction term. Extension requires lender consent and may trigger fee.
OP-R-HOLDBACKHoldback Release — Final 10% draw released only after 55-day lien period expires (BC) and title confirmed clear. Not a repayment — it is the last advance.

Collateral Structure

OP-C-1STFirst Charge Construction Mortgage — Registered in priority on title. All other charges must be subordinated or discharged.
OP-C-ASSIGNAssignment of Contracts — GC contract, architect agreement, presale contracts, and permits assigned to lender as additional security.
OP-C-PGPersonal Guarantee — Full personal guarantee from all principals. Some lenders accept completion guarantee only (limited to project completion, not repayment).
OP-C-PPSAPPSA — Personal Property Security Act registration on construction materials, equipment on site, and any chattels. Supplements the land mortgage.
OP-C-2NDSecond Charge — Mezzanine or subordinate lender registered in second position. Requires inter-creditor agreement and first-lender consent.

Monitoring & Administration

OP-M-QSQS Monitoring — Quantity Surveyor appointed by lender (at borrower's cost) to inspect and certify each draw. QS reports are the primary control mechanism.
OP-M-DRAWDraw Administration — Formal draw request process: borrower submits draw request → QS inspects → lender reviews → solicitor confirms title clear → advance released.
OP-M-SITESite Inspection Rights — Lender (or agent) has right to inspect site at any time during construction. Failure to provide access is an event of default.
OP-M-CTCCost-to-Complete Monitoring — QS tracks cumulative spend vs. budget at each draw. Overruns must be funded from equity or approved contingency before next advance.
OP-M-SCHEDSchedule Monitoring — Construction schedule reviewed at each draw. Delays >30 days require revised schedule sign-off. Delays >90 days may require a formal loan extension and additional interest reserve.

Risk / Workflow Status

OP-S-ACTIVEActive — Construction on schedule, draws advancing, no flags.
OP-S-WATCHWatchlist — Minor schedule delay or cost creep. Monitoring intensified. No draw holds yet.
OP-S-HOLDDraw Hold — One or more draw-level holds are active. Advance suspended until cleared.
OP-S-OVERRUNCost Overrun — Total project cost exceeds approved TPC. Borrower must inject equity before any further advances.
OP-S-DEFAULTEvent of Default — Borrower in breach of construction mortgage. Remedies may include appointing a receiver to complete the project.
OP-S-EXTENDExtension Requested — Loan term extension pending lender approval. Requires updated QS report, revised schedule, and extension fee (typically 0.5–1.0% of loan).
OP-S-MATUREDMatured / Exit — Construction complete. Loan repaid from sale proceeds or take-out financing. File closed.

Covenant Triggers

OP-CV-LTCLTC Covenant — At no point during draws may the cumulative advanced amount exceed the approved LTC ratio. Any cost overrun that pushes LTC above threshold is an immediate equity call.
OP-CV-LTGDVLoan-to-GDV — Total facility as a % of as-complete appraised value. Must remain within approved threshold at all times. Material decline in market values may trigger a reappraisal requirement.
OP-CV-EQEquity Maintenance — Borrower must maintain minimum equity (liquid reserves) throughout construction. Typically 10–15% of outstanding loan balance. Covenant breach triggers an equity cure period.
OP-CV-PRESALEPre-Sale Covenant — If lender required pre-sales, borrower must maintain the approved pre-sale threshold throughout construction. Cancellation of presale contracts must be disclosed and may trigger a review.

Credit unions and alt/MIC lenders are filling the gap but at meaningfully higher rates.
